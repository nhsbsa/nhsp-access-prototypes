What NHS Login is

NHS login makes it easier and quicker for you to securely access digital health and care services with 1 username and password.

The first time you set up your NHS login, you will need to prove who you are. This is so we can connect you to your NHS record and protect your health information.

When you have proved who you are, you will be able to use your NHS login to access websites and apps wherever you see 'NHS login'.

Find out more information about NHS Login.

What GOV.UK Verify is

To use some digital services, the government needs to check you are who you say you are. GOV.UK Verify gives you simple access to services like checking your state pension or driving licence details. 

It’s safe and secure

GOV.UK Verify has been developed by the Government Digital Service (GDS) and meets the highest levels of international standards for security and data protection. With GOV.UK Verify, you can be confident your identity is protected.

Partner companies assure your identity. This means the government never needs to see your personal information and the partner companies never know which service you’re using.

It gives you access to services you use

You only need to register with GOV.UK Verify once. You’ll then be able to quickly and easily access government services. You’ll be able to securely log in with your username.

It’s simple to use

  1. You’ll be asked some questions to work out which partner companies are most likely to be able to verify your identity
  2. You then choose from a list of carefully selected partner companies who can verify your identity
  3. They ask you some questions to establish it’s really you. For example, you might be asked for information about your passport or driving licence

None of this data is seen by government or stored centrally. It’s the best way to protect your information and to keep things simple, safe and secure.

There are a number of ways our partners check your identity including: driver’s licence, passport or answering a question based on your credit history.

There’s more information about GOV.UK Verify available on their website.

To access your TRS using ESR Self Service, log in to ESR and use the 'Total Reward Statement' link.

What ESR Employee Self Service is

The Electronic Staff Record (ESR) is an integrated HR and payroll system for NHS organisations in England and Wales.

ESR allows NHS employees to view and maintain their personal information, and develop their professional knowledge. 

Not all NHS Employers offer access to ESR, ask your employer if you would like more information.

You can get help with accessing your statement via ESR Employee Self Service

Haven't received a TRS Statement

You might not receive a statement if:

  • your employer is not currently ‘live’
  • you are not a member of the NHS Pension Scheme
  • you are a new member of the NHS Pension Scheme with less than one year’s service
  • you have less than one year’s service with your current employer
  • you are no longer employed by the NHS and are in receipt of your NHS Pension
  • your employer does not use ESR and you are not a member of the NHS Pension Scheme

Cannot view your NHS Pension information

Your NHS Pension information might not be available if you're :

  • not a member of the NHS Pension Scheme
  • a new member of the NHS Pension Scheme with less than one year’s service
  • already receiving an NHS Pension

There may be an issue calculating you NHS Pension using the automated system. Contact your employer for an estimate of your NHS Pension benefits.
 

Cannot view your employment information

You might not be able to view your employment information if:

  • your employer does not use ESR and your employment details are not available
  • you started your NHS employment during the current financial year, as statement information is always as at the end of the previous financial year
  • bank posts are not currently included on the statements

Your employer will let you know when a new statement becomes available.
